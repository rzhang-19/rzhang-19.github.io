1. Introduction: What is League of Legends?

League of Legends (LoL) is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed by Riot Games. Released in October 2009, League of Legends has been around for many years and has millions of players. League of Legends hit peak concurrent players of 8 million and its competitive scene peaked at 44 million concurrent viewers during 2019.

The premise of the League's primary game, Summoner's Rift, is simple: each game consists of two teams of 5 players where players are traditionally assigned a role. There are 5 roles, Top, Jungle, Middle, Bottom and Support, with various characters (champions) to pick from for each of the role. There are currently 161 unique champions which all have unique abilities. After each team picks their champions, players are sent to their respective regions of the map. During a match, champions gain levels by accruing experience points (XP) through killing enemies and minions that spawn in each lane and the jungle. Gold is also gained upon slaying enemies and enemy structures, which allow players to buy items. Items can be acquired to increase champions' strength and are bought in one's own base. Each match is discrete, so levels and items do not transfer between matches. The winner of each game is decided by whichever team breaks the opposing team's base structure (nexus) first. More info found here.

Summoner's Rift with Regions

League's World Champsionship Series (WCS/Worlds) is the largest annual competitive event for the game. The Worlds for 2022 started on September 29th and ended on November 5th. The event was hosted in North America and consists of games between 24 teams internationally. More information about the event can be found here.

In this tutorial, my goal is to tidy the Worlds 2022 data that was compiled by Oracleselixir who scraped data from all games throughout all events using League's match history features. The compiled data gives valuable insight into which champions were strongest in terms of win rate, kill-death ratio, and gold compared to the enemy in the same lane. These stats are used to predict which champions performed well based on their stats, as well as predict whether or not a team is more likely to win due to champion selection. Champions are all unique, and each are usually assigned to their own roles. This implies various champions will gain more xp, gold, and kills overall due to their selected role. For readers unfamiliar with League, I hope this sparks interest in potentially watching the competitive scene or even playing the game by giving insight on which champions may be particularly strong. For experienced players, I hope to show interesting statistics and further insight into the nail-biter finale of Worlds 2022.

2B. Modifying and Tidying the Data

First, we want to collapse the rows of the data to focus on primarily the Worlds 2022 event. This is done through taking only the data which has a column specifying the competitive league "WCS," which indicates the World Championship Series. Furthermore, some of the data had incomplete games when looking through the data, so I extracted only the data of the Worlds 2022 games whose data was marked as "complete" instead of having missing data.

Next, we need to compress the data about the game state, especially since many of the columns are either repetitive or too detailed to make a difference in the outcome of the game. To do this, we take the previous data table and further extract the important columns. Since we have data from both teams, any opponent data is redundant. I wanted to particularly focus on the data regarding kills, deaths, assists, major objectives during the game (baron nashor and the dragons), as well as the state of the game at 15 minutes. The reasoning behind the data at 15 minutes is because 15 minutes is typically when players start to leave their traditional roles and regions of the map. This means that at 15 minutes, we can see how well a certain champion or team performed against another during the first 15 minutes, which is also known as the "laning phase" during a League game.

Organizing and Modifying the data

So now that the data has cleaned out redundant and useless data for the most part, I want to extract two things: the data regarding a team's performance during a game, and the data regarding individual champion performance during games. Thus, I decided to split up the data into team data and champion data. I also noticed some data was all 0 in parts of each, so I opted to drop the empty data in the team and champion data tables.
